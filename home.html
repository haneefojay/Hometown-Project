Welcome to Abeokuta

Abeokuta, meaning "Under the Rock," is the capital of Ogun State, Nigeria. Renowned for Olumo Rock, a historic refuge for the Egba people, the city boasts a rich cultural heritage. It is famous for Adire (tie-dye fabric), vibrant festivals, and its thriving agricultural trade in cocoa and kola nuts. Abeokuta seamlessly blends historical landmarks with modern development, making it a center for culture, commerce, and education.

Some of the historic landmarksin Abeokuta

Olumo Rock

Olumo Rock is a mountain in south-western Nigeria. It is located in the city of Abeokuta, Ogun State, and was normally used as a natural fortress during inter-tribal warfare in the 19th century. Its patron spirit is venerated in the Yoruba religion as an orisha. The name olumo is the combination of two words: "olu" which means god/deity, and "mo" which means moulded

Centenary Hall

The Centenary Hall in Abeokuta was established in 1930. It was built to commemorate the centennial celebration of the Egba people's settlement in Abeokuta. The building also served as a testament to the resilience and prosperity of the Egba community and has since become an iconic historical landmark in the city

The Alake of Egba Palace

The Alake of Egba Palace is the home of the Alake of Egbaland, the traditional ruler of the Egba clan in Abeokuta, Nigeria. The palace includes the Egba Museum, which features ceremonial regalia from the town's past kings. The ground floor of the second palace has been renovated to chronicle the history of Abeokuta, which was founded in 1830.

Top three activities to do in Abeokuta

Climb Olumo Rock

A must-see landmark in Abeokuta, Olumo Rock offers breathtaking views of the city and a peek into the history of the Egba people. You can explore ancient caves, and inscriptions while enjoying a scenic climb.

Visit Adire Market

This market is the heart of traditional Adire fabric production. Watch the intricate dyeing process, shop for unique fabrics, and immerse yourself in the vibrant culture of local artisans.

Explore the Ake Palace

The palace of the Alake of Egbaland is a historical treasure. Learn about Yoruba history, admire the traditional architecture, and experience the rich cultural heritage of Abeokuta.
